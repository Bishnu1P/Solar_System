Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size and composition. However, it's a hostile world with a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide, surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead, and crushing atmospheric pressure. Known as the "evening star", Venus shines brightly in the sky due to its reflective clouds.

Some Fun Facts about Venus

  • Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures reaching about 475°C (900°F)—hot enough to melt lead! This is due to its thick atmosphere, which traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect.
  • A day on Venus (one full rotation on its axis) takes about 243 Earth days, while a year (one orbit around the Sun) takes only 225 Earth days. This means a day is longer than a year!
  • Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets, including Earth. If you stood on its surface, the Sun would rise in the west and set in the east.
  • Unlike many other planets, Venus has no moons or rings. It's a bit of a loner in the solar system!
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, making it the only planet named after a female deity.
