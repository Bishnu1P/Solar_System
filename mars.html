Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron-rich, rusty surface. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system, a thin carbon dioxide atmosphere, and polar ice caps. Mars is a prime candidate for future human exploration.

Some Fun Facts about Mars

  • Mars is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
  • Mars is about half the size of Earth, with a diameter of 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles).
  • A day on Mars (a "sol") is 24 hours and 37 minutes—just a bit longer than a day on Earth.
  • Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, which look like asteroids.
  • Gravity on Mars is about 38% of Earth’s.
