Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, known for its stunning and extensive ring system made of ice, dust, and rock. A gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, it has at least 145 moons, including Titan, which is larger than Mercury. Saturn’s diameter is about 9.5 times that of Earth, and it completes one rotation in just 10.7 hours, but a year takes 29.5 Earth years.

Some Fun Facts about Saturn

  • Saturn is so light for its size that it would float in water if you could find a bathtub big enough!
  • Saturn’s rings are the most famous in the Solar System and stretch over 280,000 km wide, but they are only about 10 meters thick!
  • Saturn has at least 145 moons and moonlets, and Titan, its largest moon, is bigger than Mercury and has rivers and lakes of liquid methane.
  • Winds on Saturn can reach speeds of 1,800 km/h (1,118 mph), making it one of the windiest planets in the Solar System.
  • NASA’s Cassini spacecraft ended its mission by plunging into Saturn in 2017, sending back incredible data until the very end.
