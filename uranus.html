Uranus is the 7th planet from the Sun and an ice giant, known for its striking blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has an extreme axial tilt of 97.8°, causing it to rotate on its side and experience unusual seasons. Discovered in 1781 by William Herschel Uranus has 27 moons, 13 faint rings, and the coldest temperatures in the solar system (-224°C). It takes 17 hours to complete a day and 84 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

Some Fun Facts about Uranus

  • Uranus was discovered on March 13, 1781, by William Herschel while he was surveying the night sky with a telescope, initially mistaking it for a comet before its planetary nature was confirmed.
  • Despite being farther from the Sun, Neptune isn’t as cold as Uranus. Uranus holds the record for the coldest temperatures in the solar system, reaching as low as -224°C (-371°F)!
  • A day on Uranus is just 17 hours and 14 minutes long—meaning if you lived there, your weekend would fly by!
  • Instead of naming its moons after mythological figures like other planets, Uranus’ moons are named after characters from Shakespeare and Alexander Pope’s works.
  • Uranus rotates almost completely on its side, with a tilt of 97.8°. It looks like it's rolling through space, unlike any other planet!
